Purpose

The purpose of the IT Department is to provide Phillips Health Management Services employees, and system users, high quality technical services provided by the Phillips Health Management Services Information Technology department and to assist computer users in finding the appropriate resource to answer their questions, resolve any computer or network related problems, assist with specific program needs, and aid them in finding appropriate computer related tools to accomplish their job at Phillips Health Management Services (PHMO).

Mission Statement

The mission of the Information Technology department is to facilitate the core business processes and provide dependable, scalable, and efficient IT solutions by leveraging technological advancements in IT hardware and software.

Organizational Description/Scope of Work

The IT department provides technical assistance and maintenance and oversight of PHMO’s computer network, software and hardware inventory, WIFI network, and all IT initiatives within the PHMO organization. The IT department encourages all departments to be our partners in planning for future computing environments for PHMO. We also hope that by understanding the role the IT department plays in maintaining the reliability and integrity of computer resources, our employees will be our partners in planning for future computing environments for the PHMO.

Responsibility and Role of the IT Department

The IT department determines and conceptually plans the best ways for Information technology to meet the goals of PHMO. The IT department will install, maintain, and upgrade computer-based systems, as necessary. The IT department will be responsible for researching and developing new ways to exploit IT resources to accomplish tasks in a productive and timely manner. All computing devices and applications added to the PHMO corporate network have an impact on the overall network. The IT department’s ability to properly support new devices and applications often require additional resources that may take time to acquire and install. The IT department will balance the load of services across resources, as necessary. By including the IT department in preliminary discussions, we can start preparations to support and keep projects flowing smoothly. The IT department is responsible for the following IT Resources/ Tasks:

A. Digital IPBX systems (3CX)
B. Corporate Internet Access
C. Office 365 and Email
D. Computer/Laptop Hardware
E. Server Hardware
F. Program Software
G. Copiers/Fax/Scanners – All peripherals
H. Video, Audio, and Image Projection equipment
I. Security Access control doors
J. CCTV systems
K. Corporate network (LAN) administration
L. Creation of user Accounts on IT Platforms
M. Provision of CUG service
N. Disaster Recovery Planning
O. Creation of User guides and policies for use of IT resources
P. Provide technical advice and training to PHMO staff in the operation, maintenance, and support of computer hardware and software systems.
Q. Prepare reports and make recommendation to ensure the optimum efficiency of equipment and systems in accordance with PHMO’s business needs.
R. Maintain stock of expendable and non-expendable computer equipment, materials, system, application, and supplies sufficient to ensure continuous and uninterrupted operation of systems.
S. Communicating with vendors regarding purchases of IT devices, peripherals, and consumables.
T. Maintain professional and technical knowledge by conducting research, attending seminars, education workshops, classes, and conferences and reviewing professional publications.

Software

The IT department will maintain all software which is located on PHMO on-premises or cloud sever systems directly or via 3rd party software vendors. As it pertains to software, the following procedures will be followed:
A. The IT department will maintain a current list of standard and recommended software.
B. To ensure software is compatible and not destructive to PHMO computer systems, the IT department will approve all software programs.
C. If a user is interested in software that is not on the maintained list, the user will need to request for approval for the application via their HOD. IT will review the application and advise.
D. All software installed on PHMO computers or on the servers must have a valid license.
E. Should sever-based software make a server unstable, the IT department will be responsible for restoring any data that was stored on a server that is backed up by the IT department’s backup application.
F. The IT department will monitor all software licenses to ensure compliance with the vendor’s license agreements.
G. Users may contact the IT department to obtain additional guidance, quotes, and advice on any software.

Hardware

The IT department will maintain all IT hardware which is located on the PHMO premises or given to Staff. As it pertains to hardware, the following procedures will be followed:
A. The IT department will maintain a current list of standard and recommended hardware.
B. To ensure hardware is compatible and not destructive to PHMO computer systems, the IT department will review all prospective company hardware and advise appropriately.
C. If a user is interested in hardware that is not on the maintained list, the user will need to get approval for the purchase of the Hardware via their HOD. IT will advise appropriately.
D. The IT department will be responsible for all hardware that is associated with PHMO Servers directly or via 3rd party Hardware or software vendors.
E. Types of common hardware used at the PHMO. Fully supported hardware are those hardware devices which are maintained by the IT department. The IT department recommends installs and tests for compatibility of such hardware with all supported environments and may upgrade hardware when new drivers are released or become available.

Prohibited Hardware and Software

The IT department will monitor the software and hardware installations within the PHMO corporate network, the following are prohibited hardware and software:
A. Hardware or software that makes any portion of the network unstable.
B. Hardware or software that is used for illegal purposes.
C. Hardware of software which there are licensing issues which legally prohibit its use.
D. Hardware or software may also be prohibited due to its tendency to destabilize or compromise the security of core network services.
In all cases, any hardware or software that makes any portion of the network unstable will be immediately removed from the network and the system will be restored to its standard configuration

Portable Devices

The users of portable devices (Official Laptops, Tabs and Smart Phones) are responsible for such devices and will be held accountable for the care of such devices. The term “portable device” includes but is not limited to the following:
A. Laptops
B. External hard disk drives
C. USB data devices and Peripherals
D. Smart Phones
E. Tablets
F. Access Cards
These devices should be stored properly while not in use and shall have approved anti-virus software installed and up to date (if applicable). All users are required to have proper authorization of a portable device from their HOD prior to being issued such device. The IT department will maintain a list of all issued devices and will monitor the user’s activity of such devices.

Replacement Plan

PHMO will follow a Computer Replacement Plan outlined in the IT AUP Document. This plan will provide for the systematic replacement of older computers that are issued to PHMO staff. This document (IT AUP) should be referred to for more granular information on use of PHMO IT hardware, software, and network by Staff.

Website

The company website is designed to provide company staff, Corporate clients, enrollees, and the public access to curated and updated information regarding the following.
I. Our corporate identity
II. Our Products and Services
III. Our Payment Channels
IV. Our Support Channels
V. Our Contact Channels
VI. Our Health Insurance and Telemedicine Platform

The IT department (working in tandem with the HODs) is responsible for ensuring the PHMO website is updated at least every 30 days.

All content submitted for posting on the website (Announcements, articles, or blogs) must have a stated source. All external sources must be credited.

Copyrighted material must be accompanied by a properly executed release from the author or owner. This release document must be sighted by our Legal department before the material is published on the PHMO website. No links to personal websites will be posted.

Disposal of Electronic Equipment

The IT department disposes of outdated electronic equipment. To have the IT department dispose of equipment, the user must meet certain requirements in the IT AUP document and get approval from their HOD. The IT department will keep outdated equipment in storage for up to 6 months, after which, the equipment will be disposed of at environmentally friendly electronics recycle location where the hard drives will be destroyed (if applicable).

Data Backup/Disaster Recovery Plan

The IT department provides automatic backup services for PHMO users and servers located at the PHMO corporate office and for mobile users. User data is backed up via Microsoft OneDrive. The PHMO Disaster Recovery Plan document describes the IT departments procedures for disaster recovery, as well as the process levels related to the recovering critical information. The IT department’s plan minimizes disruptions of critical functions and ensures the capability to recover operations expediently and successfully. Please refer to the PHMO Disaster Recovery Plan document for granular information

Maintenance

The IT department will schedule quarterly maintenance on the network to perform hardware & software updates and check for errors.

Security

Maintaining data security is the responsibility of the IT department. The security of the PHMO’s computer systems is a major priority of the IT Department. The following are components to how the IT Department monitors the system’s security:
A) The following applications and hardware have been setup to send notification, waring, and critical alerts to IT departmental staff to keep them informed of the status of these critical IT resources.
i) 3CX (PHMO’s Digital Phone and Call Center Application)
ii) PHMO Website
iii) Sophos XG (PHMO’s Firewall Appliance)
iv) Office 365 (PHMO’s Email and Collaboration Application Suite)
v) PHMO’s Cisco Switch (Core Networking Application at PHMO’s corporate Office)
B) Active Directory Security Measures have been implemented to manage user access to corporate data residing on User systems and the PHMO file share (residing on the PHMO on-premises server)
C) The PHMO network is equipped with a firewall to secure encrypted tunnel for remote users to gain a secure connection from outside the network.
D) The IT Manager must maintain backups as needed to recover from security threats and damage.
E) The IT department can log on to the servers and desktop systems remotely to ensure the network’s security is effective.

Remote Access

Remote access to PHMO computer systems and information is only permitted through secure, authenticated and centrally managed access methods. This access must be approved by the user’s HOD and can only be used to conduct PHMO business. The IT department will wok with users in setting up all remote access connections.

User Accounts, Email & Passwords

The IT department creates and administers all PHMO email addresses and domain accounts. Every new employee whose position requires them to use a computer or have access to IT resources on PHMO servers will be assigned a domain account and email account with appropriate login credentials at the time of employment. HR must make an appropriate request for a new user setup to the IT department to set up a new user account. All computer users must change passwords every 90 days. Each user’s computer system will prompt them to change passwords automatically. The IT department reserves the right to have users change their passwords at any time if the system has been compromised.

Internet Access

The internet provides access to a variety of information, both good and questionable value and is not a secure means of communication. It is the responsibility of the user to ensure the PHMO’s Computer, Intranet, and Internet Use Policy (IT AUP) is being followed. The IT department will monitor user activity through monitoring and filtering software to prevent access to sites which are illegal or against PHMO policies.

Teleconferencing / Audio Visual

The IT department has made the following available to staff for online conference meetings and Audio-Visual presentations
1) Microsoft Teams (Available on all company Laptops)
2) Epson Projector (Available in the Corporate office Boardroom)
IT department will provide remote and desktop assistance to all staff when using these resources.
